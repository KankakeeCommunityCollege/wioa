Workforce Services and WIOA

Kankakee Workforce Services provides comprehensive employment assistance through the Workforce Innovation & Opportunity Act of 2014 (WIOA). We help:

  • People who lost their jobs through plant closings or layoffs
  • Low-income individuals, working or not
  • Youth ages 16-24

Assistance includes programs and services that enhance employment marketability. These are offered at no cost to qualified individuals.

About WIOA

WIOA forms the basis for a unique national workforce preparation and employment system. It focuses on meeting the business need for skilled workers and the training, education, and employment needs of individuals. Through WIOA, customers can:

  • Access the information and services they need through a "one-stop" system
  • Obtain training through individual accounts that allow adult customers to "purchase" the training they determine best for them
  • Ensure that all state and local programs meet customer expectations

Fundable WIOA Programs

WIOA funds are designated for specific degree and training programs per local workforce area. In order for an individual to be funded by Kankakee Workforce Services, their degree or training program must be on the list of Illinois Workforce Development System programs. To search our list of programs:

  1. Visit the Illinois WorkNet Center WIOA Approved Training Programs Search page.
  2. Search using any keywords, training program name (nursing, welding, etc.), provider (college, university, etc.) or by location (city, ZIP code).

If you have any questions regarding a degree or training program, phone 815-802-8963/TTY 800-526-0844.
